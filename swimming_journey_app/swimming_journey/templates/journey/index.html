My Swimming Journey

A Splash of Success

Highlights

When I was a child, I never imagined I would become a swimmer someday. I was terrified of the pool because I was afraid that I might drown. I was just a kid wandering around the sports complex where my father worked. Who would have thought that the same child, so afraid of the water, would grow up to be an athlete and represent my school?

I started my swimming journey in Grade 6. I never imagined I'd actually learn to swim, as I was just watching the swimmers train in the pool. I tried it and, surprisingly, I was able to swim. So, my father suggested that I start swimming seriously. He hired a swimming coach.

When I was in high school, I started competing. At first, as expected, it wasn't very successful. But that didn't stop me from dreaming of becoming a professional swimmer someday. So, I trained hard, six times a week, to achieve my goal.

All my hard work finally paid off. I started earning medals, from bronze to gold. My school recognized my achievements and asked me to represent them in competitions in other regions. I started with three medals and eventually collected six in a single competition.

Sadly, my swimming journey ended when I started senior high school, as I lacked the time to balance studies and swimming. But I'm truly happy and content with everything I've experienced. All my achievements, hard work, and experiences have shaped my career. I hope my story will inspire others, including my future children.
